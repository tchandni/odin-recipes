Masala Chai

About The Dish

Masala chai is a warm, aromatic tea made with black tea, milk, sugar, and a blend of fragrant spices. It’s comforting, soothing, and deeply flavourful—perfect for mornings, rainy days, or any time you need a cozy break. Every household makes it slightly differently, but this recipe gives you a balanced, classic cup with the perfect mix of spice and sweetness.

The blend of ginger, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon creates a bold aroma, while the slow simmer with milk gives it that rich, creamy texture. Simple ingredients, big flavour—pure desi comfort in a cup.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup milk (or more if you want extra creamy)
  • 2 tsp loose black tea (or 2 tea bags)
  • 2 to 3 green cardamom pods (slightly crushed)
  • 1 small piece cinnamon stick
  • 2–3 cloves
  • ½ inch fresh ginger, crushed
  • 2–3 tsp sugar (adjust to taste)

Steps

  1. Boil the Spices
    • In a small pot, add water, ginger, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon.
    • Bring to a boil and let it simmer for 2 to 3 minutes to release the flavours.

  2. Add Tea
    • Add the loose tea or tea bags..
    • Simmer for another 1 to 2 minutes until the water turns deep brown..

  3. Add Milk
    • Pour in the milk and stir.
    • Allow the chai to come to a gentle boil.
    • Reduce heat and simmer for 2–3 minutes for a stronger, richer flavour.

  4. Sweeten
    • Add sugar and mix until fully dissolved.

  5. Strain & Serve
    • Strain the chai into cups.
    • Serve hot and enjoy the beautiful aroma.

Enjoy!
